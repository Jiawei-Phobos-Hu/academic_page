About

I am a PhD candidate in School of Information at University of Michigan, where I am advised by Daniel Romero. I am interested in computational social science, science of science, social networks, and complex systems. My research studies the mechanisms involved in the production and dissemination of science and innovation. I obtained a master's degree in Data Science from Indiana University Bloomington, where I worked with Yong-Yeol Ahn and Filippo Menczer at the Center for Complex Networks and Systems Research. I received my bachelor's degree in Information Management & System from Sun Yat-sen University.
